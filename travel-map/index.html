Welcome!

Serendipity struck when two events collided earlier this year — my start-up wound down operations, and my lease ended.

After much agonising (soul-searching?) and encouraging nudges from my support circle, I shipped most of my stuff to my folks in Chennai, stashed the rest with pals in Singapore, and decided to take off for a month in Bali.

Back in Chennai, unpacking my life’s possessions and confronting how I felt after a month off — I knew I wanted more out of this time off, and wondered what it would look like to extend to the end of the year.

Cue the budget-crunching, spreadsheet-wrangling, and flight-search frenzy. With friends and family offering couches worldwide — it all began to fall into place.

Follow the rest on the map!

Why did I build this?

  • I wanted a visually compelling way to share where all I’d been this year, and what I had done
  • I love nerding out over maps
  • I hadn’t actually gone through the motions of trying to build a website *entirely* on my own, and thought this would be a fun project to take on
  • I’d been reading loads about using LLMs to build prototypes, and wanted to see what I could do paired with an AI co-pilot (thanks, Claude!)

So, let me know what you think!

A few highlights so far:

  • Diving in the Coral Triangle, with some of the richest marine biodiversity in the world
  • Eating loads of peak summer strawberries in the UK
  • Completing an epic 3 day hut-to-hut hike in the Dolomites
  • Kayaking with sea otters in an estuary in Monterey Bay
  • Road tripping through several incredible US National Parks — Glacier, Zion, Bryce, Arches
  • A whirlwind stopover in Istanbul, a decade after I’d spent a semester studying there
